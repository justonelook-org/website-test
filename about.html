What is the Just One Look Method?

The Just One Look Method is an extremely simple approach to mental suffering unlike anything you have ever tried. It will rid you of the root cause of your dissatisfaction with life and the painful yearning for peace and fulfillment that seems never to be fully satisfied.

The Just One Look Method is the result of more than twenty years of experience working with people around the world who have seen their relationship with their own lives change dramatically for the better.

The method consists of two parts.

The first step is a simple act of inward looking called "Look at Yourself." The purpose of this act is to direct attention to the felt sense of being here as you — the simple me-ness that is present throughout your life.

The second step is the Self-Directed Attention Exercise (SDA). This exercise develops the ability to direct and focus attention deliberately. It is used to support the changes that follow the inward look and to help cultivate greater self-reliance and intelligent use of attention.

Together, these two steps form the Just One Look Method.

What Is Happening Now?

The Just One Look Foundation concluded its work in 2025.

Today, the Just One Look Method continues through the efforts of a small international team dedicated to preserving, developing, and sharing the method.

The team's primary focus is maintaining access to the method, making original materials available, and exploring new ways of sharing the work through modern technology.

The Just One Look website remains the central hub for information, resources, and guidance related to the method.

What Is the Purpose of this Website?

This website exists to make the Just One Look Method freely available to anyone interested in exploring it.

This website provides access to the method itself, original source materials, news, resources, and guidance related to the work.

As the work continues to develop, the website is evolving as well. The current approach places greater emphasis on direct access for visitors to try the method for themselves, before anything else.

The goal is not simply to learn about the Just One Look Method, but to make it easy to encounter and explore it directly.

At the same time, the website serves as a home for the original materials and a place where the ongoing development of the work can be documented and shared.

As new technologies emerge, the Just One Look team will continue to explore new ways of making the method accessible while preserving the original work.
