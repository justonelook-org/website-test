What is the Just One Look Method?

The Just One Look Method is an extremely simple approach to mental suffering unlike anything you have ever tried. It will rid you of the root cause of your dissatisfaction with life and the painful yearning for peace and fulfillment that seems never to be fully satisfied.

The Just One Look Method is the result of more than twenty years of experience working with people around the world who have seen their relationship with their own lives change dramatically for the better.

The method consists of two parts.

The first and most important step is a simple act of inward looking called "Look at Yourself." The purpose of this act is to bring your felt sense of self — the simple me-ness that has been present throughout your life — into direct experience by looking directly at it with your attention.

The second step is the Self-Directed Attention Exercise. This exercise develops the ability to direct and focus attention deliberately. It is used to support the changes that follow the inward look and to help cultivate greater self-reliance and intelligent use of attention.

Together, these two steps form the Just One Look Method.

What Is Happening Now?

The Just One Look Foundation concluded its work in 2025.

Today, the Just One Look Method continues through the efforts of a small international team dedicated to preserving, developing, and sharing the work.

This website serves as the central home of the Just One Look Method. Here you will find access to the method itself, original source materials, support, news, resources, and ongoing developments related to the work.

A New Approach

This new and updated website is built on one simple idea: that the invitation to Look at Yourself is presented first.

Visitors are invited to try it before reading about it.

The intention is to make the inward look as accessible as possible.

All material on the website is considered important. But first and foremost, the website offers a direct invitation to Look at Yourself.
